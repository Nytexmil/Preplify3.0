SAT vs ACT

by Michael Armstrong

Posted on January 1, 2021 at 12:00 PM

I’m not one of those naturally high scorers. My PSAT score in 10th grade was a 1240. After spending months of preparation, I’ve finally gotten a 1560. I mainly struggled on the English section and breezed through the math section which is the easiest or most masterable section. For all those out there who struggle in English and breeze through the math, this is the resource page for you!! This article is primarily intended for students in the 1100-1400s range. If I was able to turn my score around, there is no reason why you can’t. This article will go through the topic about score ranges and the looming question of whether you should retake the SAT.


Contents

  • Standardized Tests and their Meaning
  • Differences By Section

Standardized Tests and their Meaning

If you're looking to see which test is easier, there is no general answer. Both are equivalent in difficult for the average population, however there are key differences which may affect your performance.

A standardized test has a purpose of comparing you to students from across the nation, and thus if one was easier, its scores wouldn't be valued as much, so let's dig into which one you should take.



Differences By Section:

Reading

If you struggle in Reading, this section could be a game changer, so let's compare the reading sections.

SAT Qualities
More Time With 52 questions in 65 minutes. Although this is because of question difficulty, you get more time to read the passsages. If you are slow at reading or skimming, this is one reason to consider SAT
More Complicated Passages The SAT reading section is the hardest section on the whole test to master. The passages are more difficult to comphrend and the questions are also more inference-type rather than straight from the text. If you struggle with comphrension, it may be a reason to consider the ACT. However, it is masterable with enough effort.
Deeper and more subjective-like questions The questions have similar answer choices, and students often struggle to pick the right one. This requires deeper practice, but the curve is more forgiving as a result compared to the ACT. If you make silly mistakes a lot, maybe prefer the SAT because you can miss 5-7 and still get a 350/400.
Science Passage Majority 3 of the 5 passages are definitely either science or social science. If you're good at nonfiction passages, you may want to consider the SAT. Although the ACT also has a devoted section for scientific experiment comphrension: the sciecne section. See how you perform on the Reading + Science Section and compare them to your reading score and weight which works better.
Weightage The SAT reading section is out of 400 points, which is 25% of the total 1600 possible points. If you're bad at Reading, I suggest the SAT because the ACT has another section called the science section, which is just reading comphrension for science.

ACT Qualities
Less Time With 40 questions in just 35 minutes, this section requires extremely quick reading. Although the questions are more straightforward, teaching yourself to read faster is a tough skill. So I recommend that you give it a shot and see if you can read fast enough. The SAT is better in this department.
Passages The passages are of generally equivalent difficulty compared to the SAT. Although time is much less, so you have to comphrend much quicker. Again, if you're slow, give SAT a point.
Surface Level and Objective Questions The questions are much more straightforward, but stil require good comphrension. If you usually take your time with questions and need to verify or properly eliminate and check the passage alot, you might want to go with the SAT; on the ACT you won't have much time to decide.
Preparation The ACT reading is masterable, but when under extreme time pressure, it's sometimes difficult to apply what you have learned. The SAT reading is harder to master, but it's easier to apply what you learned due to the extra time.
Weightage There is one reading section and one science section which is heavy in reading comphrension. This totals to 50% of the test, therefore I would reccommend SAT if you're bad at Reading.


Writing/English Section

SAT Qualities
More Time With 44 questions in 35 minutes. Although this is because of question difficulty, you get more time to read the passsages and think about the questions.
More Complicated Questions The SAT writing section is extremeley masterable, HOWEVER, there is a subset of questions that require native familiarity. This involves idioms or word choice, something that you develop over time with intuition. If you aren't a native speaker, this stop you from maxing your score.
Predictable Passage Themes After doing 5-10 real practice test, you'll start seeing patterns, this is often what drives improvement to the point of missing ~3 questions
Weightage Equal to ACT weightage.

ACT Qualities
Less Time A whopping 75 questions in just 45 minutes!!! The pacing is much quicker, and you are still expected to comphrend the passage and answer similar questions to the SAT. Timing wise, this section is brutal. Missing even a couple of questions will take your score down to 33.
Less Complicated Passages The passages are more straightforward, but less predictable. You regardless have to comphrend them in higher time pressure.
Surface Level and Basic Grammar is More Prevalent There are more grammar based questions but they are still imbedded with tricks to knock of points on your score.
Weightage Same as SAT


Math Section

SAT Qualities
Weightage On the SAT, math is weighted at 50%, more than the 25% on the ACT
Difficulty Much easier than the ACT. Basic topics.
Word Problems The problems aren't as straightforward, but they have more basic topics as mentioned before. More on the concept, less with the numbers/calculation
Preparation Easier to prepare for since the topics are from lower level classes like Algebra I and Geometry
Timing Total of 58 questions in 80 minutes. Less time pressure than the SAT, despite the easier questions.


ACT Qualities
Weightage On the SAT, math is weighted at 50%, more than the 25% on the ACT
Difficulty Harder than the SAT. Tests Trigonometry and some Precalc topics
Word Problems Problems are more straightforward, but harder concepts
Preparation Harder to prepare for since the content tested is more vast.
Timing 60 questions in 60 minutes. More time pressure than the SAT, despite the harder questions.


How to Decide

In the end, you need to consider the differences above and take a diagnostic test. Naturally, you will score low in both tests before preparing. Don't just compare the scores though, you want to consider scores you could have after preparing: this can be done by looking at the differences above and judging which test matches you better.
